How to Play

What is Fizzy Sums?

Fizzy Sums is a number game that is similar to kakuro or sudoku that focuses on basic arithmetic. The goal of the game is to satisfy all the equations in the rows and columns such that each number 1-9 is used exactly once. There are many forms of this game available on the internet under different names, but Fizzy Sums follows these guidelines:

  • Order of operations (PEMDAS) applies.
  • Basic fractions may occur within the equation (1/4, 1/2, 2/3, etc.).
  • Negative numbers may occur within the equation.
For example:
  • 9+8*5=49 because you evaluate multiplication (8*5) before addition (9+8). So you get 9+8*5=9+40=49.
  • 2/8*4=1 is valid because even though you get the fraction 1/4 when dividing 2/8, after multiplying by 4 you end up with a whole number. Only whole numbers occur after the = sign.
  • 3-4*1=-1 is valid even though a negative number occurs. Some other forms of this game found on the internet may not use negative numbers but Fizzy Sums does.
